Getting started

Three steps to get you up and running.

1

Create your magicplan account

Download the app from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store and open it. Select the sign-up option, enter your email, and create a password. Agree to the terms and conditions to complete registration.

For a full walkthrough including signing up via the magicplan Cloud, visit help.magicplan.app/create-your-magicplan-account.
2

Switch to your company workspace

If your team is already using magicplan, you will receive an email invitation to join your company workspace — this is the most common way new team members get set up. Accept the invite and you'll be added automatically. Once in the app, tap the workspace name at the top left of the home screen to confirm you're in the correct company workspace. You only have to do this once.

3

Create your first project

Tap the + sign on the home screen → New Project. Name it using your team's convention (typically: owner name + address + claim number). Add the address using geolocation or enter it manually. Then tap + next to Floor Plans to start the floor plan.

During training, prefix your project name with "Test" + your first name. Your solutions consultant can credit those back after the session.

Auto scanning

The core workflow for building an instant floor plan in the field.

1

Confirm your device compatibility

magicplan's AutoScan feature requires an iPhone 12 Pro or newer, or an iPad Pro (2020 generation or newer). Non-Pro devices can still use Manual Scan with corner detection, photo documentation, connecting a 360 camera, and all other core functionality.

For a full list of supported devices, visit help.magicplan.app/supported-devices.
2

Prepare the space

Turn on all lights. In a power-out scenario, use a portable battery-powered floodlight — the scan will still work in dim conditions, but maximum light gives the best result. Close doors to rooms you're not yet scanning.

2

Tap the red record button, then select the room type

Go to Insert → Room → Auto Scan and tap the red record button to start. Then select the room type (kitchen, bedroom, bathroom, etc.). This ensures any photos you take during the scan are automatically labeled and associated to the correct room in your photo report.

3

Move slowly — sweep the device up and down

Move around the room at a moderate pace, tilting the device up toward the ceiling and down toward the floor as you go. This gives the app enough data to accurately detect walls, windows, and ceiling height.

Toggle the 2D view (button next to the record button) to see a map of what you've captured so far and confirm no walls are missing.
5

Scan section by section — not the whole floor at once

After finishing one room or area, tap the red record button to stop and confirm the scan. Then move to the next section and tap "Scan another room." Only tap Done when you've finished the entire floor. The app assembles the connected floor plan using GPS.

Scanning more than 2,000 sq ft in a single session will strain the app. Break very large floors into two sessions and join them in the editor.
6

Leave closets and pantries closed

Small closets and pantries are full of shelves and clutter, making it hard for LiDAR to measure depth accurately. Leave them closed during the scan. After, you can fill them in with the Filler Room tool in about 10 seconds. Exception: walk-in closets large enough to stand in can be scanned normally.

7

Take photos and dictate notes while scanning

Use the shutter button (above the record button) to take photos mid-scan — they auto-tag to the current room. Use the microphone button (above the shutter) to dictate a voice note describing what you observe. It transcribes automatically and attaches to the room.

Voice transcript is a public beta feature. Ask your solutions consultant to enable it for your account.
8

Choose which objects to include when finishing

After tapping Done, a window lets you include or exclude object categories (furniture, appliances, plumbing fixtures). Furniture rarely needs to come through to Xactimate — deselect it and check "Remember my choices" so it stays off by default.

Save scan recording videos. They're stored in the cloud at no extra cost and can be valuable if a dispute arises later.

Editing

How to verify, adjust, and complete your scan in the editor.

Manual room methods

  • Add square room — drops an 8×2 default; drag to resize or verify with your Bluetooth laser
  • Draw room — tap points to map any irregular shape
  • Filler room — fills structural gaps in the floor plan instantly
  • Filler wall — use if filler room snaps to the wrong wall

Key editor actions

  • Select a wall → drag to resize
  • Add corner — push or pull a section of wall
  • Edit layout — reposition or rotate entire rooms
  • Long press a room to enter edit layout mode
1

Pair your Bluetooth laser

To verify measurements, open any dimension in the editor and tap "Laser." The app detects your device make and model. Tap Connect. After the first pairing, it reconnects automatically whenever the laser is on — you never have to think about it again.

2

Verify dimensions with your Bluetooth laser

Tap any blue dimension on the floor plan, open your laser, and click once. The measurement locks with a padlock icon. Verify any dimension critical to your estimate — the scan is fast and accurate, but a laser gives you certainty.

3

Split open-concept rooms

If a kitchen, dining room, and living room scanned together: select a wall point → Split Room → delete the dividing wall that appears. Repeat for each split. Then open each section's Details panel and change the room type. They'll now have separate labels and surface area calculations.

4

Fill in closets after scanning

Insert → Room → Add Filler → Filler Room → Closet. Drop the bullseye indicator into the gap. If filler room tries to fill the wrong area, use Filler Wall instead, then open Details and change the room type to Closet.

5

Use elevation view for windows and cabinets

Switch to Elevation View at the bottom of the screen to see walls vertically. You can verify "distance from floor" for windows and reposition cabinets with precision. Cycle between walls using the left/right arrows. Often overlooked — highly useful for estimate accuracy.

6

Open the Details panel for any element

iPad: tap the eye icon at top right. iPhone: swipe up from the bottom of the screen. Details let you change room type, set ceiling height, adjust wall thickness, attach photos or notes, and add affected areas for scoping.

Photos, videos & 360s

Building a complete, organized photo report alongside your floor plan.

1

Attach photos at the right level

Photos can be attached at the project level (exterior shots), floor level, room level, or even to individual walls and objects. Use project-level for exterior documentation, room-level for damage documentation, and object-level for specific elements like windows or cabinets.

2

Annotate and redact within the app

After taking photos, tap Next → Edit. Use arrows to indicate source of loss, add text boxes, draw shapes, and change annotation colors. Use the redaction tool to blur family portraits or sensitive information before anything goes into the report. Tap Save All when done.

3

Tag every photo Beta

After photos, tap Tags to apply labels like "pre-mitigation," "daily departure," "emergency," or create a custom tag. Tags let you filter quickly when building reports on the cloud. On multi-day jobs with multiple 360s, tagging each day's scan clearly is essential.

This feature was built directly from SERVPRO feedback. It saves significant time at the reporting stage — make it a habit from day one.
4

Connect your Ricoh or Insta360 camera

In the camera app, select 360. First time only: enter the serial number from the bottom of your camera (both code sections). After that, the camera is remembered and connects automatically. Capture as many 360s as needed per session using the shutter button.

If only the first 360 uploads then stalls, first update magicplan via the App Store and restart your camera. If the issue persists, remove the camera from saved Bluetooth devices and re-pair. Still stuck? Note your Ricoh model and contact your solutions consultant.
5

Share a 360 tour with adjusters and homeowners

On the cloud, open your project photos → Export Photos → 360 Tour. Name it and publish. A shareable link is generated instantly. Anyone with the link can view the tour in a browser — no magicplan account required. Send it to insurance carriers, adjusters, or the homeowner.

Exporting files

Getting your sketch, photo report, and tour out of magicplan and into Xactimate or your inbox.

Sync before you export. magicplan works fully offline — scanning, editing, photos, 360s — but you need a data connection to generate exports. Pull down on the home screen to manually sync first. If you've been working without signal, sync when you're back online before generating any exports.
1

Export the ESX for Xactimate

From the floor plan view: tap the two squares + arrow icon at top right → Verisk. Or from project overview, tap Export on the left. Enable photo compression to maximize how many photos are included. If the export fails, toggle photos off — you've likely hit the size limit.

Xactimate limits ESX files to 100MB of photos. With compression, you can fit roughly 200 photos. Above that, exclude photos from the ESX and share them via the photo report PDF or 360 tour link instead.
2

Export the FML for Cotality

Same export menu → tap Integrations to find the FML option. You can generate and re-export as many times as needed within a project at no additional cost.

3

The file doesn't appear right away — here's why

Files are generated in the cloud, not on your device. After exporting, pull down on the home screen to sync. Go back into the project and scroll down to the File section — it'll be there. Files also appear on the magicplan Cloud.

4

Generate the PDF photo report

On the cloud: open project photos → Export Photos → PDF. Choose photos per page, toggle date/time/author display. The PDF is interactive — recipients can click embedded videos and 360s directly from the file. No app or account needed to view it.

5

Wall thickness and Xactimate alignment

If walls look misaligned in Xactimate after importing the ESX, this is a known behavior resulting from how the two platforms store floor plans differently — not a measurement error in your original sketch. Objects added in magicplan before export will import correctly and their surface area deductions will work as expected when deduction preferences are correctly configured in Xactimate.

If reference blocks won't snap to a wall, see the Recommended Solutions section of our reference guide, or add the object in magicplan and re-export the ESX.

Ask your estimator to check the default wall thickness in Xactimate settings, then match that value in magicplan at the floor level via Details → Wall Thickness. magicplan is actively working with Verisk on a long-term fix.

magicplan Cloud

What to set up once, and how your office team can access everything from a browser at cloud.magicplan.app.

1

Check your workspace when you first log in

Same as the app — you may land in your personal workspace. Click the workspace name at top left and switch to your company workspace. Do this once and it will persist.

2

Invite your whole team — it's unlimited users

Go to Members → enter email and name → assign to a team. magicplan Pro has no per-user charge, so invite everyone who will ever need to view or work on projects. Remove members from this same screen when someone leaves.

3

See all team projects

By default the cloud only shows your own projects. Click "See all projects of workspace members" to view everything your colleagues are building. Filter by team or by individual member to find what you need quickly.

4

Reassign projects between team members

Open a project → Assign To → select a colleague. They receive an email notification and can continue the work. Only the assigned owner can edit — others can view. Make sure the project is fully synced before reassigning.

Where possible, have the same person start and finish a job. Reassigning mid-job works fine, but keeping one owner reduces confusion on large projects.
5

Two settings worth turning on

In Settings on the magicplan Cloud, these two options are off by default but worth considering for most teams:

Convert 360 spherical images to cubic faces when downloading — when enabled, every 360 photo automatically generates 6 flat images (front, back, left, right, top, bottom). Useful if your team or carriers need standard flat images alongside the 360 tour, without any extra work on site.

File versioning — when enabled, each new export creates a new file rather than overwriting the previous one. Helpful if you re-export ESX or PDF files frequently and want to keep a clear history of versions — particularly useful on longer or more complex jobs.

Neither setting is required, but both are low-effort to enable and can save time down the line. Worth a conversation with your team before deciding.
6

Set up company profiles per office

In Company Profile: upload your logo and enter contact details. If you have multiple offices, create a team for each and configure a separate profile per team. Each office can have its own logo and contact information on all generated reports.
